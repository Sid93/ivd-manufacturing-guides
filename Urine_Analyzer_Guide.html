2. Technology Overview & Principles

▼

How Reflectance Photometry Works

When urine is applied to a reagent strip pad, a chemical reaction produces a color change. The analyzer shines multispectral LED lightLEDs at multiple wavelengths (Green 525nm, Red 660nm, Blue 450nm, IR 850nm) illuminate the strip pad. Different analytes absorb different wavelengths, enabling precise identification. onto each pad and measures the reflected light intensity using a photodetectorA semiconductor device (photodiode) that converts light into electrical current. The current is proportional to the intensity of reflected light hitting the sensor..

📚
Kubelka-Munk Equation: The theoretical foundation relating reflectance (R) to concentration. K/S = (1–R)² / 2R, where K = absorption coefficient and S = scattering coefficient. Higher analyte concentration → more absorption → lower reflectance.

Optical Architecture

Signal Processing Pipeline

  1. Dark Subtraction: Measure with LEDs off to get baseline noise; subtract from all readings
  2. Ratiometric Measurement: Calculate R = (Signal – Dark) / (Reference – Dark) to cancel LED drift
  3. Temperature Compensation: Apply correction: Corrected_R = Measured_R × (1 + α × (T – T_ref))
  4. Calibration Lookup: Map reflectance to concentration via polynomial curve stored in flash
  5. Classification: Convert continuous values to semiquantitative clinical categories (Negative, Trace, 1+, 2+, 3+)

💡
Why Multispectral? Different analyte-reagent reactions produce different colors. Using 4 wavelengths allows the system to distinguish between analytes and improves accuracy through ratiometric measurement.

Electronic System Architecture

Key Component Details

  • Photodetector: Silicon photodiode (e.g., BPW34 or Hamamatsu S1223-01) with spectral response covering 400–1000 nm
  • Transimpedance Amplifier: OPA380 (TI) — converts photodiode nanoamp-level current to measurable voltage
  • ADC: ADS1115 (TI) — 16-bit, 4-channel, I²C interface, 860 SPS. Provides ±0.01% accuracy
  • Optical Fiber Bundle: Bifurcated bundle — one leg delivers LED light, other leg collects reflected light. Core diameter ~1mm, NA ≈ 0.5
  • Temperature Sensor: DS18B20 (1-Wire) for real-time temperature compensation

7. Manufacturing Process

▼

6-Phase Manufacturing Flow

Phase 1: PCB Fabrication & Assembly

Order 2-layer (2P) or 4-layer (10P) PCBs from local fabricators. SMD components placed via pick-and-place or hand soldering. Reflow solder in oven. ICT (In-Circuit Test) for shorts/opens.

Phase 2: Optical Module Assembly

Mount LEDs on small PCB with precise alignment jig. Polish fiber bundle ends to optical finish. Assemble into CNC-machined aluminum optical head. Characterize each LED/photodiode pair.

Phase 3: Mechanical Assembly

Install strip channel guide with alignment pins. Mount PCB to housing with M3 standoffs. Route cables with ties. Install display, SMPS, front panel buttons. Light-leak inspection in darkened room.

Phase 4: Firmware Development & Loading

Flash STM32 firmware via ST-Link V2 programmer (₹300–600). Modules: init, LED driver, ADC sampling, optical processing, calibration, display, communication, storage, self-test.

Phase 5: Calibration & Validation

White reference calibration, dark reference, multilevel analyte standards, strip lot calibration, temperature compensation. See Section 8 for detailed protocol.

Phase 6: Final QC & Packaging

Full self-test routine. Test with 3 control samples (Neg, Low, High). Verify all parameters. Record serial/lot/calibration data. Apply CE/BIS labels. Package with accessories.

8. Calibration & Quality Control

▼

Calibration Philosophy

Calibration maps the raw reflectance signal (R) to semiquantitative results. It must account for four key variation sources:

  1. Lot-to-lot strip variation — different reagent batches absorb differently
  2. Instrument-to-instrument optical variation — LED intensity, fiber transmission differences
  3. Temperature effects — reaction rates change with temperature
  4. Aging effects — LED intensity drifts over time

Factory Calibration Protocol (5 Steps)

Step 1: White Reference Calibration

Measure reflectance of a NIST-traceable white ceramic tile (R = 100%). Store as factory baseline in MCU flash for all channels.

Step 2: Dark Reference

Measure photodetector output with all LEDs off. Store as dark offset; subtract from all subsequent measurements.

Step 3: Multilevel Calibration with Aqueous Standards

Prepare 5–7 concentration levels per analyte using certified reference materials. Measure reflectance at each level. Fit polynomial: R = f(concentration). Store coefficients in flash.

Step 4: Strip Lot Calibration

Each strip lot has a calibration code (on bottle). Operator enters lot code; instrument loads appropriate offset. Compensates for inter-lot reagent variation.

Step 5: Temperature Compensation

Measure at 15°C, 25°C, 35°C. Derive linear correction coefficients per analyte: Corrected_R = Measured_R × (1 + α × (T – T_ref))

10. Regulatory Pathway (CDSCO / MDR 2017)

▼

Regulatory Classification

Under the Medical Devices Rules (MDR) 2017, a urine dipstick analyzer is classified as an In Vitro Diagnostic (IVD) deviceA medical device intended to examine specimens derived from the human body (blood, urine, tissue) to provide information for diagnosis, monitoring, or treatment..

ComponentClassificationRisk Level
Urine Analyzer (Instrument)Class B IVD deviceModerate risk
Reagent StripsClass B IVD deviceModerate risk
⚠️
Both the instrument and the strips require separate CDSCO registration before commercial sale in India.

5-Step Regulatory Pathway

Step 1: Manufacturing License (SLA)

Apply to State Licensing Authority under Form MD-3 (Class B). Requires GMP-compliant facility inspection. Fee: ~₹3,000–5,000. Timeline: 3–6 months.

Step 2: CDSCO Device Registration

File on SUGAM portal (cdsco.gov.in). Submit Device Master File (DMF): design specs, manufacturing process, Risk Management File (ISO 14971), Performance Evaluation Data, Biocompatibility (ISO 10993), Labeling/IFU, QMS certificate (ISO 13485). Form MD-7. Fee: ₹2,500/device. Timeline: 6–12 months.

Step 3: ISO 13485 Certification

Quality Management System certification. Required for export and preferred by institutional buyers. Certification bodies: BSI, TÜV SÜD, Bureau Veritas. Cost: ₹3–8 lakh. Timeline: 6–12 months.

Step 4: BIS Certification (If Applicable)

Bureau of Indian Standards mark may be required. Check mandatory BIS list for IVD devices. Apply under IS/IEC 61010-1 (safety for lab equipment).

Step 5: Labeling Requirements

Per MDR 2017 Schedule V: Device name, model, lot number, serial number, manufacturer details, manufacturing date, shelf life, “For In Vitro Diagnostic Use Only”, CDSCO registration number, storage conditions, ISO 15223-1 symbols.

14. Glossary & References

▼

Key Terms

Reflectance Photometry
Measurement technique that quantifies analyte concentration by measuring the intensity of light reflected from a colored reagent pad.
Kubelka-Munk (K-M)
Mathematical model relating reflectance to absorption and scattering coefficients: K/S = (1-R)²/2R. Foundation of strip-based colorimetric analysis.
Transimpedance Amplifier (TIA)
An op-amp circuit that converts the tiny photocurrent from a photodiode into a measurable voltage signal. Critical analog front-end component.
ADS1115
A 16-bit, 4-channel analog-to-digital converter by TI with I²C interface. Provides high-resolution digitization of the TIA output signal.
STM32
A family of ARM Cortex-M microcontrollers by STMicroelectronics. F103 (Cortex-M3) for 2P model, F407 (Cortex-M4 with FPU) for 10P model.
Semiquantitative
Results expressed in discrete categories (Negative, Trace, 1+, 2+, 3+) rather than exact numeric concentrations. Standard for dipstick urinalysis.
CDSCO
Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation — India’s national regulatory authority for drugs and medical devices under the Ministry of Health.
MDR 2017
Medical Devices Rules 2017 — Indian regulation governing manufacturing, import, sale, and distribution of medical devices including IVDs.
ISO 13485
International quality management system standard specifically for medical device manufacturers. Required for export and institutional sales.
IVD Device
In Vitro Diagnostic device — medical device used to examine specimens from the human body (blood, urine) for diagnostic purposes.
Ratiometric Measurement
Calculating the ratio of signal to reference channels to cancel out common-mode noise like LED drift and power supply variation.
Bifurcated Fiber Bundle
An optical fiber bundle split into two legs: one delivers illumination light, the other collects reflected light. Enables compact optical head design.
GeM Portal
Government e-Marketplace — India’s online platform for government procurement. Registration enables sales to government hospitals and health centers.
CV (Coefficient of Variation)
A measure of precision: (Standard Deviation / Mean) × 100%. Lower CV = more consistent, reproducible results.
Reagent Rental Model
Business model where the instrument is provided free/subsidized in exchange for a minimum consumable (strip) purchase commitment. “Razor-and-blade” model.
NABL
National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories — Indian accreditation body for clinical labs. NABL labs require validated equipment.
