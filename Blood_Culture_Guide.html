1 The Big Picture 芒聙聰 Why Blood Culture Matters

▼

What is a blood culture? It's the gold-standard lab test for detecting bacteria or fungi in a patient's bloodstream. When someone develops a bloodstream infection (BSI)When bacteria or fungi enter and multiply in the blood. This can lead to sepsis 芒聙聰 a life-threatening condition where the body's response to infection damages its own organs., doctors need to know which organism is causing it so they can choose the right antibiotic. A blood culture does exactly that: a sample of blood is placed in a special bottle containing nutrient broth, and the bottle is incubated to see if anything grows.

The Sepsis Crisis in India India reports roughly 2.9 million sepsis cases per year. Hospital-acquired bloodstream infections affect about 15% of ICU patients. Yet only ~30% of suspected sepsis cases in India actually receive blood culture testing (compared to >90% in developed countries). This massive "diagnostic gap" means many patients never get the right antibiotic in time.

The market problem: Right now, over 80% of Indian hospitals still rely on old-fashioned manual broth cultures 芒聙聰 pouring blood into a flask and visually checking it every day. The automated systems that exist (BD BACTEC and bioM脙漏rieux BacT/ALERT) are imported, expensive, and mostly available only in Tier-1 city private hospitals.
